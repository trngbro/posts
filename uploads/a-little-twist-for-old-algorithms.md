Lately I’ve been brushing up on some of the classic algorithms – you know, those you once studied hard for exams, but over time they somehow slipped into the foggy corners of memory.

Instead of just reading walls of text or re-watching lectures, I thought: *why not try something different this time?* So I grabbed my notebook, scribbled step by step, and then suddenly it hit me:  
👉 Why not **draw them out**? Visualizing an algorithm doesn’t just make it easier to understand; it actually makes the process fun.

---

## Why I started this

I realized that when I only look at pseudocode or long explanations, my brain zones out pretty quickly. But when I try to picture how the data “moves,” “changes,” or “flows,” suddenly everything clicks.

So I began tinkering with tiny visualizations. Some are very rough, almost like doodles, but they work!
- They help me remember the flow better.
- They make the abstract steps concrete.
- And honestly, they make algorithms feel less intimidating.

---

## Sharing the journey

At first, it was just a personal exercise. But then I thought: if these little sketches can help me, maybe they could help others too. After all, algorithms are not only about passing exams or acing interviews — they’re the backbone of problem-solving in computer science.

That’s why I’ve started publishing them little by little on my own product page:  
👉 [**algorithm.ntncorner.works**](https://algorithm.ntncorner.works)

You’re welcome to drop by and take a peek. Who knows, maybe you’ll find a visualization that makes a concept finally “click” for you too. 👀
